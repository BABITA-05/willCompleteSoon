Upcoming Events

TechParva 2026

Date: To be announced (Pokhara, Nepal) Organized by the Innovative Computer Engineering Students’ Society (i-CES), TechParva is one of Nepal’s most impactful student-led technology festivals. With the bold theme “Bigger • Bolder • Better”, it brings together competitions, workshops, and industry engagement to empower young technologists. Students can showcase innovations, collaborate with peers, and gain real-world exposure to Nepal’s growing tech ecosystem.

Locus 2026

Date: January 30 – February 1, 2026 (Pulchowk Campus, Lalitpur) LOCUS is Nepal’s largest national-level technological exhibition, led by undergraduate students of Electrical, Electronics, and Computer Engineering at IOE, Pulchowk Campus. Since 2003, it has been a platform for innovators from schools and colleges across Nepal to present projects, compete in challenges, and attend workshops. The 22nd edition in 2026 continues its mission of fostering collaboration, promoting individual development, and driving innovation.

Past Events

Presidential Innovista 2025

Date: August 7–10, 2025 Location: Presidential Graduate School, Kathmandu This was Nepal’s largest tech carnival, showcasing over 50 student-led projects across IT, business, and entrepreneurship. Innovista blended cutting-edge fields like AI, cybersecurity, robotics, and multimedia with practical business applications. Tech students gained hands-on exposure to real-world challenges, while non-tech students explored innovation management, pitching, and commercialization strategies. It created a vibrant platform for interdisciplinary collaboration and future-ready skills.

DevFest Kathmandu 2024

Date: December 2024 Location: Kathmandu, Nepal Organized by Google Developer Groups (GDG), DevFest Kathmandu was a community-driven conference that brought together developers, designers, entrepreneurs, and students from diverse backgrounds. Sessions covered cloud computing, web development, AI, and startup culture. Tech students benefited from coding workshops and technical talks, while non-tech participants engaged in discussions on innovation, leadership, and networking. It was a gateway to connect Nepal’s student community with global technology trends.
